Design System Guide
Hotel Darstein — Lakeside Wedding Elegance

A premium lakeside wedding hotel, designed around romance, calm luxury, and complete celebration.

The website should feel like a refined wedding destination at the Blue Adriatic: emotional first, practical second. It should sell the dream of a lakeside ceremony, then support that dream with hotel rooms, catering, planning, restaurant service, and event infrastructure.

Primary CTA: Request Wedding Viewing Secondary CTA: Explore Wedding Options

Typography Feel

Use a refined editorial contrast: elegant headings for emotion, clean body type for clarity and trust.

Your Dream Wedding Directly on the Blue Adriatic
Celebrate your ceremony, reception, dinner, party, and overnight stay in one romantic lakeside destination. Text should be warm, clear, and confident — never generic or overly sales-heavy.

Homepage Design Principle

The homepage should be built like a wedding story, not a list of hotel services.

01. Dream

Open with cinematic wedding/lake imagery and a clear emotional promise.

02. Place

Show the wedding island, lake, terrace, and indoor/outdoor celebration spaces.

03. Trust

Explain planning support, catering, guest rooms, parking, and complete event service.

04. Convert

Guide couples toward a viewing, inquiry, or individual wedding offer.

Wedding Island: The signature emotional differentiator.
Why Darstein: Lake, wedding island, hotel rooms, catering, planning, location.
Wedding Day Flow: Arrival, ceremony, reception, photos, dinner, party, overnight stay.
Spaces & Weather Confidence: Outdoor romance plus indoor event security.
Menus & Packages: Food, buffet, drinks, trial dinner, planning support.

Photography Direction

Photography should carry the emotion of the brand. Use warm, cinematic, natural images.

Must Show

Couple by the lake, wedding island, terrace reception, dinner setup, food, rooms, party atmosphere, exterior lake views.

Mood

Golden hour, soft shadows, water reflections, natural smiles, candlelight, floral details, warm hospitality.

Avoid

Cold corporate rooms, empty generic spaces, harsh lighting, over-saturated colors, stock-photo feeling.

Buttons & Conversion

Every major section should gently push users toward a wedding inquiry.

Primary CTA

Use for: Request Wedding Viewing, Send Wedding Inquiry, Request Individual Offer.

Secondary CTA

Use for: Explore Wedding Options, View Rooms, Discover Restaurant, See Event Spaces.

Microcopy

Use helpful reassurance: “Tell us your date and guest count — our team will prepare an individual offer.”

Do & Don’t

Do

  • Lead with weddings and lake emotion.
  • Use the burgundy logo identity as a premium accent.
  • Design large, calm, editorial sections.
  • Make inquiry CTAs visible throughout the page.
  • Show complete wedding-day convenience.

Don’t

  • Make the homepage feel like a generic hotel booking site.
  • Let conferences or restaurant content compete with weddings.
  • Use cold corporate colors or crowded layouts.
  • Hide the inquiry form at the very end only.
  • Rely on text when photography can sell the feeling better.

Final Creative Direction

Design Hotel Darstein as a premium wedding venue with hotel comfort behind it. The first impression should be: “This is where we can have a beautiful lakeside wedding, celebrate with our guests, and stay overnight without stress.”
